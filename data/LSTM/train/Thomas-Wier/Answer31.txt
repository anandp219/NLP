  Normally when linguists count the number of pronominal distinctions  they are interested in basically three features: person  number and clusivity. (The third might be considered a subclass of the first.) In many languages  pronouns inflect for other features  such as case  gender  alienability  visibility and even geographical elevation (as in some languages of Daghestan) but these are usually not considered central to linguistic theorizing. (For example  Brian Collins' list of case forms is large compared to English or Chinese  but nothing like the list of case-forms for Tabassaran  which has around 50.
