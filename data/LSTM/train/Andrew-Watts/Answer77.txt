The writer Hugo Ball  himself a prominent practitioner  said in a 1917 lecture that painter and art theorist Wassily Kandinsky  in his book Der gelbe Klang (1912)  "was the first to discover and apply the most abstract expression of sound in language  consisting of harmonized vowels and consonants." Throughout the article Kostelanetz mentions a number of other pieces as possible influences on Schwitters either predating or concurrent with Ursonate. For instance  the German poets Paul Scheerbart and Christian Morgenstern wrote "Kikakoku!//Ekoralaps!"
