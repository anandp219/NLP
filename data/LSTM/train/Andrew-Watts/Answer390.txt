Orchestral Idioms and Mass Effects after World War II]Micropolyphony was coined by composer Gyrgy Ligeti in the early 1960s to describe the application of canonic structures to large numbers of individual instrumental or vocal lines. A prime example would be his 1961 orchestral work Atmospheres  whereby a 48 part mirror canon is constructed with separate parts for each of the fourteen first violins  fourteen second violins  ten violas  and ten cellos.
