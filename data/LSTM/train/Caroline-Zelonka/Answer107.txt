Here is an excerpt from an article she wrote  which later was incorporated into her famous book "Nickel and Dimed" (emphasis mine):But cleaning services lay down rules meant to enforce a factorylike - or even conventlike - discipline on their far-flung employees. At The Maids  there were no breaks except for a daily 10-minute stop at a convenience store for coffee or 'lunch' - meaning something like a slice of pizza. Otherwise  the time spent driving between houses was considered our 'break' and the only chance to eat  drink or (although this was also officially forbidden) smoke a cigarette.
