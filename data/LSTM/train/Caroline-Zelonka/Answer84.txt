You don't get to touch him  but I've seen him up close a million times. His skin is dark and leathery  and appears hard to the touch. Just what you might expect from dried-out skin on a pig or other hairless animal. But the fact that his teeth and soft tissue are more or less intact suggests that there is still some moisture left in his body; something the CAT scan also revealed.He still has a tongue and eyeballs  amazingly enough. In June  2015  The History Channel Canada featured Sylvester on the premier episode of its show: Mummies Alive.
