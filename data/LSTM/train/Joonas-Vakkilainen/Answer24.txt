Other t's are not assimilated with glottal stop  even though they would with some other consonants  at least in my dialect. In standard Finnish  t's are always pronounced as [t]. There are other word types  too  where a lost final consonants assimilates with the following phoneme  creating a long glottal stop before vowels. vene rannalla [ener:n:l:] ('boat on the shore')vene saapui [enes::pui] ('boat arrived')venekin [enek:in] ('boat  too')vene oli [ene:oli] ('boat was')This phonological feature is called boundary gemination (rajageminaatio in Finnish)  traditionally also end gemination (loppukahdennus) in Finnish grammar.
