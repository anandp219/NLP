Lucier's I am sitting in a room is the perfect example of what Behrman describes as dealing with the nature of sound from scratch. Furthermore  in Background Noise  Robert Ashley's work The Wolfman is portrayed in several ways as a precursor to I am sitting in a room:The stutter in this case is a form of controlled feedback: it comes back to haunt Lucier  yet to a point of comfort and composition  where the composer may reside  take up home  within his own somatic tick  similar to Robert Ashley's work The Wolfman  from 1964  where voice unleashes a form of controlled and harmonic noise.
