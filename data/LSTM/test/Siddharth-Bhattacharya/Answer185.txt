However the issue of whether any intervention if at all is required for the former kind- natural- is more contentious as it's seen as a by-product of a knowledge economy. The advocates of intervention mention the "false" assumption of level-playing field  equal opportunity and rising inequality meaning it being more difficult for relative poor to afford basic services like healthcare and education  which despite requiring expertise on the part of provider are seen as basic human needs. Both sides in such discussions have merits  and the question of where to draw the line in intervention is a challenge for the policy-makers.
