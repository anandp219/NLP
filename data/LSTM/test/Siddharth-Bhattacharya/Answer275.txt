All this undoubtedly created a strong sense of Islamic identity for the adherents  based on it's glory. But the decline continued with the growth of European powers  proselytizing by Christianity and colonization of vast areas of land erstwhile held under Islamic rule until the 17th century. What this meant was a strong opposition to accepting the ways of the conqueror and thus Islamic identity continued to develop as a negation of Western thoughts and ideas. And so at the beginning of 20th century  there were attempts at Pan-Islamism to unite Islam across the world under the Ottoman Caliphate (or Khalifa).
