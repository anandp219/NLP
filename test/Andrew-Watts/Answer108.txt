This point of extemporaneousness  without preparation is one of the key distinctions often drawn between works of improvisation and others of indeterminacy. Returning to The Oxford Companion to Music  the improvisation entry further emphasizes this detail:The implication is that improvisation is the freest kind of creative activity  in which spontaneity and lack of forethought displace the long and often tortuous processes which compositional acts are traditionally assumed to involve  as well as displacing the painstaking learning and processing of a printed text which performance normally presumes.Here are some situations to illustrate cases of difference between the two terms before examining the Coleman  Lewis  Zorn  and Riley works.
