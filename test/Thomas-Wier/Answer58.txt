For example:it doesn't always attach to the positive form but sometimes attaches to a different  irregular form (will vs. won't  shall vs. shan't);it doesn't attach to all English verbs  but only to certain auxiliaries: could/couldn't but not regular verbs like anticipate/*anticipaten't or even some auxiliaries like may/*mayn't.it doesn't attach to words that already have clitics: *I'dn't be doing that if I were you.it exhibits unpredictable semantic peculiarities. For example between different negated auxiliaries you can get different scopes of negation: you mustn't go home means that it is the case that you must not go home  while you can't go home means that it is not the case that you can go home.
