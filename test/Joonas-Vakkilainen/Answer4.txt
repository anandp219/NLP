  Six of Finnish eight vowels are arranged as pairs  where one is a back vowel and one a front vowel. The only difference between those vowels is the back-front position of the tongue. In a  your tongue is in the back position. To produce   keep the same low position as in a but push your tongue forward.The same applies for the rounded front vowels as well  which may be difficult for people who don't speak western European languages. To make   keep your lips in the same shape and the tongue in the same height as in o but push your tongue forward.
