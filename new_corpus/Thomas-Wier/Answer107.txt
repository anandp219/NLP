Third  there is a genuine problem of defining what a word is. Georgian and other Kartvelian languages are polysynthetic  in the sense that words often have six or more affixes (with a maximum of 12) for all kinds of categories: person  number  tense  aspect  mood  evidentiality  voice  etc. What in English might be several words in Georgian often becomes one word  because Georgian has so much morphology to build new words.
