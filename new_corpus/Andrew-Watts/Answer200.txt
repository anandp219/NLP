Other New York based artists soon followed suit  for example Frank Stella's Die Fahne Hoch! (1959)  Ad Reinhardt's so-called "black" paintings of the 1960s  and Agnes Martin's early 1960s square canvases works that were covered in dense  minute and softly delineated graphite grids. Therefore  it is possible that one influence on the early minimalist composers was indeed not musical  but rather this artistic parallel.It is also possible that Terry Riley was reacting against other dominant compositional styles of the time  with these musics functioning as an influence not to compose in that way  but rather try the opposite.
