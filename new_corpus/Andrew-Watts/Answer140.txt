Lucier went so far as to say: It was a product of the times  it was trendy and pretentious to make prose scores contain indeterminate aspects [] it was a mistake. I believe Lucier's true intent for how the work should exist can be seen in the composer's own performances. He is not laissez faire about the resulting sound  as the open instructions may have one to believe. Instead  it is clear Lucier is looking for a certain  highly particular result.
