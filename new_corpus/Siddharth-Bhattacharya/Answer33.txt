He also makes some marvelous offers difficult to refuse. The reader is enamored by his charm. Gambling is after all not such a huge moral issue. And then slowly Puzo unveils what these offers were- involving bloodshed  killing- deciding who was worth living (Fannucci). Nevertheless Vito's survival arguments makes us willing to accept this new dimension of him. He represents what we want to be- powerful person  who in his deathbed could say "Life is Beautiful".
