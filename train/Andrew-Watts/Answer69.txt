Bach's school boy days at Eisenach:[...] Research begun in the 1930s in the town archives by Hermann Helmbold  and in the 1990s by Rainer Kaiser  paints a very different picture  suggesting that the Eisenach boys of the time were typical ruffians: rowdy  subversive  thuggish  beer- and wine-loving  girl-chasing  known for breaking windows and brandishing their daggers to impress.It seems that in adulthood the composer's penchant to imbibe did not at all subside.
