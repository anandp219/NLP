Regardless  as a youth training as a chorister Ockeghem would have likely been familiar with the first generation of Franco-Flemish Composers  performing their works frequently.As Ockeghem matured in his musical abilities he was appointed to the court of Charles I  Duke of Bourbon  whose principal residence was in Moulins. According to Perkins  Ockeghem was listed first among the seven signer of the ducal chapel  preceded only by the premier chaplain and three priests  indicating that his performance skills were highly valued.
