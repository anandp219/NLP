  When people speak of "old languages"  they usually mean two different things: how conservative the language is  and how long it has been written  or a combination of both. The article The 10 Oldest Languages Still Spoken In The World Today has been going around social media recently  and it focuses on the two points I mentioned in the first paragraph. Many times these kinds of articles are complete non-sense  published by journalists with no linguistic knowledge (many scientific reporters have a background in natural sciences  which is why they can't evaluate linguistics).
